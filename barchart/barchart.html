Country Comparison Bar Chart

Instructions: Use the radio buttons to select which audio feature to display. The bar chart shows the mean value for each country. Click on any bar to see the full distribution (histogram) of that feature for the selected country on the right. This brush and link interaction lets you explore the spread of values behind each average.

Click a bar to see the distribution

Description: The bar chart displays the mean value of a selected audio feature for each of the 10 European countries, with countries on the x-axis and values on the y-axis. Since this is a bar chart, the graph uses bars as marks to represent the data. For channels, the graph uses the length of each bar to encode the mean value of the selected feature, where a taller bar indicates a higher average. The bars are colored in a consistent blue, with the selected country highlighted in coral to indicate an active selection. The radio buttons allow switching between nine audio features: danceability, energy, speechiness, acousticness, instrumentalness, liveness, valence, tempo, and duration. Clicking on any bar triggers a brush and link interaction, revealing a histogram on the right that shows the full distribution of the selected feature for that country. This linked view enables multi-factor analysis by letting the user examine the spread and shape of the underlying data behind each average. For example, two countries may have similar mean danceability, but their distributions may differ in spread or skewness, revealing deeper differences in listening patterns.
